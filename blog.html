Why semantic html matters

Published: February 14, 2026· Day 6

When I first started, I used div for everything. Header? div. Navigation? div. Main content? div. Footer? also div. My code looked like a pile of boxes with no labels.

Then the teacher asked: "How does someone reading your code know what's what? How does a screen reader know where the navigation is?"

That's when I discovered semantic html.

What I learned:

  • Header - for introductory content, logos,navigation
  • Nav - specifically for navigation links
  • main - the main content of the page
  • article - self-contained content like blog posts
  • section - groups related content
  • aside - sidebar, related info
  • footer - bottom of page, copyright, contact

Why it matters:

  1. Accessibility: screen readers rely on semantic tags to navigate. a blind user can jump from header to main to footer because of these tags.
  2. SEO: search engines understand your content better. they know what's navigation vs what's the main article.
  3. Readability: other developers (and future me) can read the code and understand the structure immediately.

Now I build every website with semantic tags. My NGO website has a header,nav,main with section for programs, article for each program, and a footer. It feels clean. It feels right.

The project I'm most proud of

Published: February 15, 2026 · Day 7

I've built 7 projects this week. Flower websites, netflix clones, e-commerce stores. But the one I keep coming back to? the recipe planner.

Why? because it solves a real problem.

Every day, people including me, stare into their fridge and ask "What's for dinner?" and then we order takeout or let food go bad. The recipe planner lets you track what's in your pantry, plan meals for the week and see what you can cook with what you have.

Features I built:

  • Pantry inventory: A table showing ingredients, quantities and expiry dates.
  • Weekly meal planner: A full table from monday to sunday with breakfast, lunch, dinner.
  • Popular recipes: Rcipes that are popular and easy to follow.
  • Substitution guide: What to use when you're missing an ingredient.
  • Food waste tracker: Showing how much waste you've prevented.

Why I'm proud:

This isn't just a clone. It's an original idea. I thought about what people actually need, and I built it. Yes, it's just html, but the structure is there. The thinking is there. When I add Javascript later, this will actually work.
